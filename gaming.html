KEY DIFFERENCES

  1. Focus on Global/Regional Clients Instead of Domestic Mega Platforms

    2. China: Strong focus on a massive domestic digital ecosystem (e.g. Taobao, Douyin, Xiaohongshu), alongside growing international expansion.
    Singapore: Focus on regional (Southeast Asia/APAC) and global business markets.

    Singapore firms usually serve:

    • Southeast Asia/APAC markets
    • International B2B and B2C clients


    Examples:
    Construct Digital
    Verz Design is known for integrated digital marketing and UI/UX services.

    This contrasts with China's digital marketing ecosystem, which is heavily driven by its large domestic user base and platform-centric commerce environment, though leading firms are increasingly expanding globally.

  2. Different Platform Ecosystem and Data Environment

    3. China: Platform-centric ecosystem (e.g. WeChat, Alibaba, Douyin) with highly integrated services and strong data capabilities.
    Singapore: Open internet environment with stricter data privacy regulations (e.g. PDPA).

    China's marketing industry benefits from tightly integrated platforms where payments, social media, and e-commerce are combined, enabling highly targeted campaigns.

    Singapore operates within a more fragmented, global internet ecosystem and under stricter data protection laws, which affects how data can be collected and used.
    Campaigns often need to work across multiple platforms with comparatively less centralised data access.

  3. Less Reliance on KOL/KOC Culture

    4. China: Social commerce is highly influencer-driven and deeply integrated into platforms.
    Singapore: Uses a more balanced marketing mix, including influencer marketing.

    Singapore uses influencer marketing too, but:

    • generally less intensive than in China,
    • more brand- and campaign-driven,
    • less dependent on fully integrated social commerce ecosystems.


    Social commerce exists in Singapore but is less embedded into the end-to-end purchasing journey compared to platforms like Xiaohongshu or Douyin, where KOL and KOC-driven commerce plays a central role.

  4. Smaller Teams, Broader Roles

    5. China: Larger, more specialised teams operating at scale.
    Singapore: Leaner teams with more cross-functional roles.

    Singapore companies often expect:

    • multi-skilled employees,
    • leaner team structures,
    • broader responsibilities across functions.


    This reflects a focus on efficiency and productivity, shaped by Singapore's smaller talent pool and higher operating costs.
